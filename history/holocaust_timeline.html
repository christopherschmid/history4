Holocaust Timeline

1933:

  • Hitler becomes Chancellor of Germany.
  • Dachau concentration camp is established.

1938:

  • Kristallnacht (Night of Broken Glass) takes place.

1939:

  • Germany invades Poland, starting World War II.
  • Euthanasia programs targeting disabled individuals begin.

1941:

  • Mass shootings of Jews in the Soviet territories begin.
  • The United States enters World War II.

1942:

  • The Wannsee Conference outlines the "Final Solution."
  • Concentration and extermination camps become operational.

1945:

  • Auschwitz is liberated in January.
  • Allied forces liberate Buchenwald in April.
  • Hitler commits suicide on April 30.
  • Nazi Germany surrenders on May 7.

The Holocaust resulted in the systematic murder of millions, mainly Jews, and serves as a grim reminder of the horrors of prejudice and discrimination.
