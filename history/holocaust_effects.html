Immediate Effects of the Holocaust

  • Liberation and relief efforts for survivors.
  • Nuremberg Trials to prosecute war criminals.
  • Establishment of Displaced Persons (DP) camps.
  • Struggles with repatriation and reunification of families.

Long-Term Effects of the Holocaust

  • Creation of the state of Israel.
  • Enduring memory and Holocaust education.
  • Ongoing psychological trauma for survivors and descendants.
  • Focus on human rights and genocide prevention.
  • A lasting reminder of the dangers of prejudice and hatred.
